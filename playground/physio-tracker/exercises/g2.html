Complete ACL Prehab Library

Perform these movements slowly and with control. Red highlights the active moving segments.

Phase 1: Range of Motion & Activation

1. Heel Slides

Slowly slide your heel toward your glutes, keeping it on the floor. Hold, then slide back.

2. Quad Sets

Squeeze the thigh to push the back of the knee flat against the floor.

3. Terminal Knee Ext. (TKE)

Attach a band behind the knee. Squeeze the quad to push the knee back, locking it straight.

4. Prone Knee Hangs

Lie face down with your knees off a bed. Let gravity pull your legs perfectly straight.

Phase 2: Strength (The Muscle Splint)

5. Straight Leg Raise

Lock the knee fully straight (quad set), then lift the leg 12 inches. Lower slowly.

6. Glute Bridges

Drive through your heels and squeeze your glutes/hamstrings to lift your hips.

7. Clamshells

Lie on your side, knees bent. Keep feet together and lift the top knee like a clamshell.

8. Long Arc Quads

Sit on a chair. Slowly straighten the injured leg, squeeze at the top, and lower.

9. Hamstring Curls

Stand tall. Bend your knee, bringing your heel straight up toward your glutes.

Phase 3: Stability & Balance

10. Single-Leg Stance

Balance on the injured leg. Keep hips level and use arms for slight balance if needed.

11. Monster Walks

Mini-squat position. Step sideways, driving off the hip to activate the glute medius.

12. Bird-Dog

Tabletop position. Extend the opposite arm and leg perfectly straight, engaging the core.
